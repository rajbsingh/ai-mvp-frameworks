# AI Startup Validation Checklist

This checklist helps founders validate an AI startup idea before spending too much time or money on development.

AI products should not be built only because the technology is exciting. They should be built because users have a real problem and are willing to adopt or pay for a solution.

---

## 1. Problem Validation

Questions:

* Is the problem real?
* Who has this problem?
* How often does it happen?
* How painful is it?
* What happens if the problem is not solved?
* Are users already trying to solve it manually?
* Are users already paying for alternatives?

---

## 2. User Validation

Questions:

* Who is the first target user?
* Is the user clearly defined?
* Can the user explain the problem easily?
* Does the user have budget or influence?
* Is the user easy to reach?
* Can the user become an early adopter?

---

## 3. Market Validation

Questions:

* Is the market large enough?
* Is the market growing?
* Are there existing competitors?
* What are competitors missing?
* Is there room for a focused niche product?
* Can this become a larger platform later?

---

## 4. AI Fit Validation

Not every product needs AI.

Questions:

* Why is AI useful here?
* Does AI reduce time, cost, risk, or complexity?
* Does the workflow need summarization, generation, classification, retrieval, or automation?
* Would a simple software workflow be enough?
* Can AI output be trusted with human review?

---

## 5. Data and Knowledge Validation

AI products often depend on data quality.

Questions:

* What data does the product need?
* Is the data available?
* Is the data sensitive?
* Who owns the data?
* Does the product need documents, databases, APIs, or user input?
* Are there privacy or compliance risks?

---

## 6. MVP Validation

Questions:

* What is the smallest useful version?
* Can the first version be tested manually?
* Can users understand the value quickly?
* Can the MVP be built without complex integrations?
* Can the MVP be demoed before full development?
* What must be excluded from version one?

---

## 7. Willingness to Pay

Questions:

* Would users pay for this?
* How much time or money does the problem cost today?
* Who approves payment?
* Is this a must-have or nice-to-have?
* Can the product create measurable value?
* Can it support subscription pricing?

---

## 8. Risk Validation

Questions:

* What can go wrong?
* Is the use case legally sensitive?
* Is human review needed?
* Can wrong output harm users?
* Are there security risks?
* Is there regulatory exposure?
* Can the product be safely positioned as an assistant rather than final authority?

---

## 9. Go / No-Go Decision

Proceed only when:

* The problem is clear
* The target user is specific
* The workflow is valuable
* AI has a strong role
* Data requirements are realistic
* Risk can be controlled
* Users show interest
* The MVP can be built simply

---

## Conclusion

Validation protects founders from building products nobody wants.

A good AI startup begins with a painful user problem, not only with a powerful AI model.
